Rules

  1. Before you can do anything besides read the rules, you must login.
    • Usernames must be between 3 and 20 characters and must be only letters or numbers.
    • Passwords have the same constraints as usernames.
    • Passwords are salted and stored as hashes. They are not stored as plain text.
    • Optionally store an email!

  2. Before you can play you must Submit Questions.
    • Submitted questions will be linked to your username and will be used in any games you join.
    • One question will be used for each round of play, and the game ends when there are fewer than four questions remaining.
    • If you want to have a long lasting game, make sure to submit lots of questions!

  3. Create a game and get ready to play.
    • One player chooses a name and creates the game.
    • All other players join the created game by entering the game's name.
    • If you exited the game, you can rejoin to re-enter a game you are already playing
    • When you join a game, choose a pseudonym to use. This will be the name other players see as you play your game.
    • You must have at least three players to begin a game. Five or six players is recommended.

  4. The Game Play, get excited.
    • One player is the Questioner, she chooses the question the other players will answer.
    • One player is the Reader, he will read the answers annonomously to the Questioner.
    • All other players are Regular Players, they answer the question chosen by the Questioner.
    • When all players, except the Questioner, have answered the question, the Reader reads all the answers out loud. He does not read who wrote the question.
    • The Questioner must guess who wrote each answer.
    • If the Questioner guesses a player correctly, the Reader places a check next to that player's name. One point is awarded for each correct guess.
    • At the end of a round, the Questioner becomes the Reader, the Reader becomes a Regular Player, and one regular player becomes the Questioner.

  5. Enjoy the game. More information below:
    • If you would like to see how we store information, or just look at the code, find it on GitHub!
    • If you have questions or concerns, email them to poseidon@uglywalrus.com.
